Importance

Why Is Physical Activity Important?

Back to Intervention Access

Great news: regular physical activity provides major health benefits, even when your weight stays the same. While activity supports weight loss, that is only one of many reasons to move.

  • Physical activity amplifies weight-loss efforts when paired with nutrition changes.
  • It helps prevent future weight gain.
  • It reduces the risk and progression of chronic disease—even without weight loss.
  • Vigorous activity improves insulin sensitivity, blood pressure, and body composition.

Movement also boosts mood, energy, social connection, and enjoyment. These benefits generally outweigh temporary discomforts such as soreness or fear of injury. Find reasons that matter to you personally and keep them front of mind.

Despite these advantages, most adults with obesity do not yet meet the 150 minutes per week of moderate-intensity activity recommended by public health guidelines. Understanding the "why" is a powerful step—this intervention will help you turn that knowledge into action.
